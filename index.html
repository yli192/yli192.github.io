Gary Y. Li, Ph.D.


About me

I am a Research Scientist at Philips Research in Cambridge, MA. Previously, I was a postdoc at the MGH/HMS Center for Advanced Medical Computing and Analysis. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Johns Hopkins University, advised by Prof. Eric Frey in 2020. Before that, I obtained my B.S. in Radiological Engieering and a minor in Physics from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

My ultimate objective in my career is to remain an engaged researcher in the realm of medical imaging and AI, acting as a conduit for the translation of research findings in medical AI to clinical applications. During my Ph.D., I designed and studied anthropomorphic model observers that can be used as surrogate for radiologist to perform clinical detect detection tasks in renal SPECT imaging. In summer 2018, I was a deep learning research intern at IBM Watson Research, mentored by Drs. Mehdi Moradi, Alexandros Karagyris, and Tanveer Syeda-Mahmood. My current role at Philips is focused on developing novel algorithms for computer-assisted disease detection and diagnosis for Lumify handheld ultrasound.

Prior to graduate school, I worked on developing virtual reality (VR) courses with Prof. Rizwan Uddin. These VR courses are now part of our department's curriculum and had been used by incoming freshmen as supplements to the real experiments since their first launch in 2012.
